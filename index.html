Project Description

The Baxter Creek Watershed Alliance (BCWA) is a federally incorporated non-profit organization dedicated to local watershed conservation and engaging the public in their efforts. The BCWA utilizes Esri software to facilitate their work, allowing for the creation of user-friendly GIS applications and maps for acquiring and displaying spatial data. Through this technology, the BCWA focuses its conservation and community connection initiatives on the Baxter Creek sub-watershed, a tributary of the Otonabee River that flows through Peterborough.

The BCWA has employed Survey123, an Esri tool. This platform enables them to efficiently gather information on various aspects of the watershed. Through the collaboration with Fleming College students, two key surveys, the Amphibian Monitoring survey and the Watershed Watch survey were created using Survey123, allowing both the BCWA and the public to contribute valuable findings in the watershed ecosystem. A quality control dashboard process was also created for administrative review of the data before publishing to the public.

With Fleming College, the BCWA has developed an extensive set of educational resources freely accessible to the public. These resources include an engaging ArcGIS StoryMap that showcases the project deliverables and provides information on how individuals can actively support the BCWA's efforts. Additionally, the BCWA has produced an educational map series featuring an Indigenous Engagement, LiDAR, Reference, and Geology Map, which offer strong visual and relational understandings of the watershed. Finally, a Watershed Characterization Dashboard was created to provide an overview of the environmental characteristics of the watershed.

Throughout the collaborative project, the products created foster a greater understanding of the Baxter Creek sub-watershed's ecological significance, educates the public about watershed health, and empowers individuals to contribute to the conservation efforts. By using the power of GIS technology and knowledge of its functionality, the BCWA and Fleming College were able to establish an innovative and effective approach to watershed management, ensuring a sustainable future for the Baxter Creek and its surrounding ecosystem.
